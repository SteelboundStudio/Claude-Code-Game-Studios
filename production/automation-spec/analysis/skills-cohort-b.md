# Skills Cohort B — HITL Gate Analysis

Scope: 36 skills (qa-plan, quick-design, regression-suite, release-checklist, retrospective, reverse-document, review-all-gdds, scope-check, security-audit, setup-engine, skill-improve, skill-test, smoke-check, soak-test, sprint-plan, sprint-status, start, story-done, story-readiness, team-audio, team-combat, team-level, team-live-ops, team-narrative, team-polish, team-qa, team-release, team-ui, tech-debt, test-evidence-review, test-flakiness, test-helpers, test-setup, ux-design, ux-review, vertical-slice).

Goal: KEEP humans in CREATIVE/TASTE/JUDGMENT; AUTOMATE pure TECHNICAL/MECHANICAL/VERIFICATION/COORDINATION — never by deleting the check, but by replacing the human approval gate with an automated check that preserves verification.

Gate types: VERIFICATION (objective pass/fail) · MECHANICAL (write/format/status) · COORDINATION (planning/tracking logistics) · JUDGMENT (quality/fun verdict) · CREATIVE (taste/design authoring).

Key principle applied throughout: separate the **write mechanism** (mechanical → automatable) from the **content being gated**. When the content under the gate is AI-authored creative/design material, the approval IS the taste checkpoint → KEEP-HITL. When the content is computed from objective inputs, the approval is mechanical → AUTOMATE with a schema/completeness/lint replacement check.

## Notes

### Skills with NO HITL gate (already autonomous)
- **scope-check** — `allowed-tools: Read, Glob, Grep, Bash`; read-only; verdict computed from a net-change-% threshold table. Zero approval gates, zero writes. Fully autonomous.
- **sprint-status** — `allowed-tools: Read, Glob, Grep`; explicitly "read-only… never proposes changes, never asks to write." Burndown and stale-story detection are deterministic computations. Fully autonomous.
- (Near-autonomous, read-only, but each emits a JUDGMENT/VERIFICATION verdict and makes a non-blocking "want help?" offer — not a true approval gate: **story-readiness**, **ux-review**. Their *verdicts* are advisory; their completeness checks are automatable, but the player-need/AC-quality judgment in each keeps them HYBRID rather than fully autonomous.)

### AMBIGUOUS cases needing owner confirmation
- **story-done Phase 5 (Lead Programmer Code Review Gate) + team-combat Phase 2 (architecture gate)** — code/architecture review mixes objective static-analysis (automatable as blocking lint) with genuine design-judgment ("is this the right abstraction"). Owner must decide how much code review to replace with a blocking linter vs. reserve for human. Marked HYBRID; confirm the linter ruleset before automating.
- **smoke-check Phase 4 + team-qa Phase 5 (manual smoke / manual QA batches)** — these gate on a human *observing the running game*. They are VERIFICATION in spirit but currently un-automatable without an e2e/integration harness. Owner must decide whether to invest in scripted e2e smoke tests (which would let these go AUTOMATE) or keep the human observation gate. Until the harness exists, KEEP the observation portion.
- **team-release Phase 6 production deploy** — tag/changelog/staging are mechanically automatable, but production deployment is irreversible. Owner should confirm whether to retain a human authorization step on the final production push even after an automated GO, or allow full auto-deploy.
- **sprint-plan Phase 4 / story-done Phase 4b (Producer / QA "CONCERNS" branches)** — the UNREALISTIC/INADEQUATE branches are objective (capacity math, coverage math) and automatable; the CONCERNS branch is a risk-tolerance judgment. Owner should confirm the default behavior on CONCERNS (auto-proceed-with-log vs. always-ask).
